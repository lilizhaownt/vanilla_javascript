Steps to achieving your financial dreams

What are your financial dreams? To own a house? To pay off your debt? To retire early and live a financially indepedent life? Regardless of your earning, you can achieve your financial dreams with budgetting and investing. Let's plan for your financial future together!

Step 1: Set your financial goals

It is hard to achieve a goal without writing it down. List your finacial goals from the easiest to the hardest to achieve. Focus on the easiest goal first and delete them as you accomplish them.

Step 2: How much debt do you have?

There are two kinds of debt: good and bad. Good debt is what you must have in order to build wealth. For example, a mortage is a good debt because it allows you to buy a house. A house often increase in value over the years. Another example of good debt is a business loan. Bad debts are often a result bad spending habits. An example of bad debt is credit card debt that comes with high interest rates. Whether it is a good debt or bad debt, debt prevents you from having true financial freedom. To pay off your debt fast, attack the debt with the highest interest rate first.

Step 3: Set your budget

With your financial goals in mind, it is important to budget your income in order to reach those goals. Your budget should include money to pay for your expenses, debt, and emergency fund. An aggressive budget would be 50% expense /30% debt reduction /20% emergency saving. We recommend at least 30% of your total income should go toward debt reduction and an emergency saving.

Why do should you include emergency saving as part of your budget? Life is not perfect. We all encountered challenges such as loosing a job, injuries, illness, accidents, or car repairs. An emergency saving prevents you from going into debt during those unfortunate life events. How much emergency saving is enough? We recommend that you start with 3 months worth of expenses in mind. You can increase the amount as your finacial circumstances improve.

When you set your budget, it is important to keep in mind where your money is coming from? Do you have a regular job that pays weekly, biweekly, or monthly? Do you get paid by commission where some months you would not have an income? Set your budget to reflect your income circumstances. For instances, set a weekly budget if you get paid weekly from a regular job.

Step 5: Seek help if you cannot meet your needs

It is perfectly normal to need help. If your income cannot cover your weekly NEEDS (not WANTS!) and that you cannot increase your income, you should reach out to your local charities. Below are a list of non-profit organizations that can help you get through your challenges.

  • Make use of your local Food banks to help you acchieve your expense budget.
  • Need clothes? Salvation Army gives out good quality donated clothes.
  • Need to see a doctor but have no insurance. Check out your local Free Clinics.
  • Need help with rent? Consumer Finance can help.
